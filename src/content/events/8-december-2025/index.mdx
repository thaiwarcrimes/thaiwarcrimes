## Morning Attacks and Escalation in An Ses Area (8 December 2025)

On **8 December 2025**, Thai attacks continued into the early morning hours, beginning with force movements and heavy fire directed at Cambodian positions.

At **4:00 AM**, **two heavy Thai military vehicles** transported forces from **Phnom Roeng** into the **An Ses area**.

At approximately **5:04 AM**, Thai forces attacked Cambodian troops in **An Ses**, firing **multiple tank rounds** at:
- **Tamone Thom Temple**
- **5 Makara**
- **Chomka Chek**

From **5:04 AM to 5:25 AM**, Thai soldiers fired **dozens of small arms and machine gun rounds** at Cambodian positions.

At **5:40 AM**, firing temporarily stopped.

## Renewed Heavy Fire and Temple Attacks (6:13 AM – 7:40 AM)

At **6:13 AM**, Thai forces fired **MT-60 guns**, followed by **four mortar rounds at 6:22 AM**.

At **6:51 AM**, Thai tanks attacked **Tamone Temple**.

By **7:40 AM**, Thai forces shelled multiple locations using **155mm heavy artillery, tanks, and mortars**, targeting:
- **Mom Bei**
- **An Ses**
- **Phnom Khmoch**
- **Koul 8**
- **Preah Vihear Temple**
- **Tamone Temple**
- **Ta Krabey Temple**
- **Khna Temple**

## Use of Toxic Gas and Continued Shelling (8:10 AM – 8:41 AM)

At **8:10 AM**, Thai forces **deployed toxic gas** in **Phnom Khmoch**.

By **8:14 AM**, additional **toxic gas** was sprayed near **Preah Vihear Temple**.

At **8:41 AM**, shelling continued at **Domnak Sdech**, between **Thmar Don** and **Tamone Temple**.

## Air and Ground Assaults Using F-16 Jets (9:05 AM – 10:00 AM)

From **9:05 AM to 10:00 AM**, Thai forces launched combined attacks using:
- **F-16 fighter jets**
- **Tanks**
- **Mortars**
- **Heavy artillery**

Targets included:
- **Sra Em Commune**
- **Khna Temple**
- **Ou Smach**
- **An Ses**
- **Preah Vihear Temple**

## Expanded Attacks in Military Region 4 (11:50 AM – 5:05 PM)

In **Military Region 4**, Thai attacks intensified:

- **11:50 AM**: Drone attacks began  
- **12:20 PM**: Drones bombed **seven locations**:
  - Preah Vihear Temple
  - 5 Makara
  - Ta Thav
  - Phnom Khmoch
  - An Ses
  - Mom Bei
  - Koul 8
- **1:02 PM – 1:05 PM**: **F-16 jets dropped nine bombs** on **Mom Bei** and **Ta Krabey Temple**
- **1:30 PM**: Artillery, tanks, and mortars struck **Ta Krabey** and **Khna Temples**
- **2:24 PM**: Explosions occurred in **An Ses**, **Damnak Chongdai**, and **Ang Tek**
- **3:30 PM**: Thai forces attempted to capture **High Point 677** in **An Ses** using infantry, tanks, and drones; **Cambodian forces successfully defended the position**

## Evening Bombardment and Continued Shelling (5:05 PM – 8:40 PM)

From **5:05 PM to 8:40 PM**, Thai forces shelled a wide range of locations using **artillery, mortars, tanks, drones, and F-16 jets**, including:
- **An Ses**
- **Phnom Khmoch**
- **Preah Vihear**
- **Koul 8**
- **Mom Bei**
- **O’ Smach**
- **Khna Temple**
- **Ta Krabey Temple**
- **Thmor Don**
- **Chob Angkunh**
- **Tamone Temple**

## Attacks in Military Region 5 – Civilian Areas (1:36 PM – 7:05 PM)

In **Military Region 5**, Thai forces targeted **Prey Chan Village**, O’ Bei Chorn Commune, O’ Chrov District, **Banteay Meanchey Province**:

- **1:36 PM – 4:27 PM**: Thai forces fired **artillery, mortars, machine guns, and handguns**, moved **armored vehicles**, laid **barbed wire**, and **destroyed Cambodian observation cameras**
- By **7:05 PM**, the situation in **Chok Chey** and **Prey Chan villages** had **temporarily stabilized**

## Cambodia’s Official Position

The Royal Government of Cambodia:
- Strongly condemns the widespread and coordinated Thai attacks  
- Notes the use of **heavy weapons, air power, drones, and toxic gas**  
- Reaffirms that all targeted locations lie within **Cambodian sovereign territory**  
- Commends Cambodian forces for disciplined defense and protection of key positions  
- Calls for accountability and adherence to international law  

## Conclusion

The events of **8 December 2025** represent one of the most extensive and intense Thai military offensives against Cambodian territory, involving simultaneous ground, air, and drone operations across multiple regions. The targeting of temples, strategic positions, and civilian-adjacent areas constituted a grave escalation and a serious breach of international norms, while Cambodian forces maintained defensive posture and successfully prevented territorial seizure.
