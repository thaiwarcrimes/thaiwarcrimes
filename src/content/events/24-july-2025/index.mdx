## Escalation into Open Conflict (24 July 2025)

On **24 July 2025**, tensions along the Cambodia–Thailand border escalated into **open armed conflict** following a sequence of coordinated military actions carried out by Thai forces in sensitive and disputed areas under Cambodian sovereignty.

## Initial Violation at Ta Moan Thom Temple (6:30 AM)

At approximately **6:30 AM**, Thai military forces **violated previously agreed arrangements** by ascending **Ta Moan Thom Temple** and deploying **barbed wire at the base of the temple**.

This action constituted:
- A breach of bilateral understandings  
- An infringement on Cambodian sovereignty  
- A direct provocation in a highly sensitive cultural and religious site  

## Aerial Reconnaissance and Opening Fire (7:04 AM – 8:30 AM)

At **7:04 AM**, Thai forces launched a **reconnaissance drone** over the Ta Moan Thom area, further escalating tensions and signaling imminent military action.

By **8:30 AM**, Thai forces **opened fire** on Cambodian troops stationed in defensive positions near Ta Moan Thom Temple.

## Full-Scale Assault and Expansion of Hostilities (8:46 AM)

At **8:46 AM**, Thai invading forces initiated a **full-scale assault** on Cambodian troops at **Ta Moan Thom Temple**.

The attack rapidly expanded beyond the initial site to include:
- **Ta Krabey Temple**
- **Phnom Khmao (Black Mountain)**
- **The Mom Bei area**

These coordinated attacks indicated a deliberate and premeditated escalation rather than an isolated incident.

## Airstrike Near Wat Kaew Seekha Kiri Svarak Pagoda (10:40 AM)

At approximately **10:40 AM**, the conflict escalated further when a **Thai F-16 fighter jet** dropped **two bombs** near the road leading to **Wat Kaew Seekha Kiri Svarak Pagoda**, a sacred Cambodian religious site.

This airstrike represented:
- A major escalation in the use of force  
- A serious threat to civilian and religious infrastructure  
- A grave violation of international norms and Cambodian sovereignty  

## Cambodia’s Official Position

The Royal Government of Cambodia:
- Strongly condemns the Thai military’s actions  
- Affirms that Cambodian forces acted strictly in **defensive response**  
- Reiterates its commitment to peaceful resolution and international law  
- Reserves the inherent right to protect national sovereignty and territorial integrity  

## Conclusion

The events of **24 July 2025** marked a decisive and dangerous escalation of the Cambodia–Thailand border conflict. Thailand’s coordinated ground assault, aerial reconnaissance, and use of air power against Cambodian positions and religious sites constituted a serious breach of bilateral agreements, international law, and regional stability, significantly increasing the risk of broader conflict.
