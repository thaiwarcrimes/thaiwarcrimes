## Overview

Between April 22 and April 28, 2011, intense fighting erupted along the Cambodia-Thailand border in the Dang Rek Mountains region. This represented one of the most serious military confrontations between the two nations in decades.

## The Fighting

The clashes involved:

- Heavy artillery bombardment from both sides
- Mortar fire targeting military positions
- Small arms exchanges between infantry units
- Reported use of rocket-propelled grenades
- Fighting across multiple sectors of the disputed border

## Civilian Impact

The conflict had devastating effects on local populations:

### Casualties
- At least 15 civilians killed, including children
- Dozens of civilians injured by artillery fire and unexploded ordnance
- Several soldiers killed or wounded on both sides

### Displacement
- Over 15,000 Cambodian civilians evacuated from border villages
- Thousands of Thai residents relocated from affected areas
- Temporary displacement camps established in both countries

### Infrastructure Damage
- Schools, homes, and temples damaged by artillery
- Land contamination from unexploded ordnance
- Disruption of agricultural activities and livelihoods

## International Concerns

Human rights organizations documented concerns about:

- The use of artillery in populated areas
- Inadequate protection measures for civilians
- Limited access for humanitarian assistance
- Potential violations of international humanitarian law

## Diplomatic Efforts

The fighting prompted emergency meetings at ASEAN and calls for:

- Immediate ceasefire
- Withdrawal of forces
- Third-party observers
- Binding mechanisms to prevent future escalation

## Current Status

A ceasefire was eventually brokered, but the underlying territorial disputes remain unresolved. The incident highlighted the urgent need for permanent border demarcation and conflict prevention mechanisms.
