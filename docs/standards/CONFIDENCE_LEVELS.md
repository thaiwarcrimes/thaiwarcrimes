# Confidence Levels & Verification Standards

This archive uses **confidence levels** to clearly communicate **how certain a claim is**.

This prevents misinformation, avoids false certainty, and builds long-term trust.

**Definition**
- Strong evidence from reliable sources

- 1 reputable source  
- No credible contradictory evidence

**Example**
> Reported by a major news outlet citing official military sources.

### ðŸŸ¡ MEDIUM CONFIDENCE
**Definition**
- Partial verification
- Information is plausible but incomplete

**Minimum requirements**
- 1 source of moderate reliability  
- Or multiple weak sources pointing to the same facts

**Required labeling**
- Must clearly state what is unknown

**Example**
> Multiple local reports describe the incident, but official confirmation is pending.

### ðŸ”´ UNVERIFIED / DISPUTED
**Definition**
- Conflicting claims

> Claims could not be independently verified and are disputed by opposing statements.

## ðŸ”„ Updating Confidence Levels

Confidence levels are **not permanent**.

They should be:
- Upgraded when new evidence appears
- Downgraded if evidence is challenged
- Revised transparently via PRs

---

## ðŸ§ª Moderator Rules

Moderators will:

## Guiding Principle

> **When in doubt, downgrade.  
> Overconfidence harms credibility more than uncertainty.**
